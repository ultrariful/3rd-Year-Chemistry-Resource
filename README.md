# 🧪 3rd-Year Chemistry Resources — University of Barishal

Welcome to the official repository for the **3rd-Year Undergraduate Chemistry Curriculum** at the **University of Barishal**. This repository serves as a centralized, curated open-education platform designed to assist undergraduate students in accessing structured academic resources, including reference textbooks, lecture notes, official presentations, and laboratory manuals.

---

## 📅 Academic Curriculum & Repository Directory

The repository is structurally organized based on the official departmental syllabus. Each course contains dedicated sub-directories partitioned into `Books`, `Notes`, and `Slides` to streamline resource allocation and navigation.

### 📘 Theoretical Courses
1. **`[CH 301] Surface Chemistry, Colloids and Phase Equilibria`** (3 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Characterization of solid surfaces, adsorption isotherms (Langmuir, Freundlich, BET), Gibbs adsorption equation, Langmuir-Blodgett films, electrical double layer, micelles, and one/two/three-component phase equilibria systems.
2. **`[CH 302] Chemical Kinetics and Photochemistry`** (3 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Integration of rate equations, steady-state approximations, collision and transition state theories, unimolecular reaction theories (Lindemann-Hinshelwood), homogeneous/heterogeneous catalysis, laws of photochemistry, and radiation chemistry foundations.
3. **`[CH 321] Chemistry of Natural Products`** (2 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Primary and secondary metabolites; isolation, purification, and structural elucidation of alkaloids (morphine, quinine), terpenoids, steroidal hormones, organic coloring materials (chlorophyll, hemoglobin), and biological pheromones.
4. **`[CH 322] Advanced Stereochemistry`** (3 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Geometric isomerism, absolute/relative configuration notation (R/S, D/L), chiral and prochiral molecules, conformational analysis of cyclic compounds and biopolymers (carbohydrates, proteins), chirality devoid of chiral centers, and optical rotatory dispersion (ORD/CD).
5. **`[CH 323] Organic Reagents and Syntheses`** (2 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Advanced oxidation/reduction mechanisms, functional group protection strategies, organometallic reagents (Grignard, Organo-Copper/Zinc), modern coupling reactions (Heck, Suzuki, Sonogashira), and retrosynthetic disconnection approaches.
6. **`[CH 341] Transition Metals and Coordination Chemistry`** (3 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Magnetism in 3d and 4f elements, lanthanide contraction, coordination theories (Werner, VBT, CFT, LFT, MOT), crystal field stabilizing energies, Jahn-Teller distortion, and mechanisms of ligand substitution reactions.
7. **`[CH 342] Inorganic Process Industries`** (2 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Industrial unit operations, chemical manufacturing processes in Bangladesh (Chlor-alkali, NPK fertilizers, Portland cement parameters, glass/ceramics matrix design, and blast furnace iron/steel metallurgy).
8. **`[CH 361] Chemical Spectroscopy`** (3 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Electromagnetic radiation interaction with matter, rotational (microwave) spectroscopy, vibrational infrared (IR/FTIR) fundamentals, Raman scattering, electronic UV-Visible transitions (Franck-Condon principle), and magnetic resonance principles (NMR and ESR).
9. **`[CH 362] Chromatographic Methods`** (2 Credits)
   * *Core Focus:* Theoretical parameters of chemical separation, column chromatography, Ion-Exchange processes, High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC parameters), thin-layer planar techniques (TLC), Gel permeation, Gas Chromatography (GC), and coupled hyphenated techniques (GC-MS).
10. **`[CH 371] Quantum Chemistry and Statistical Mechanics`** (3 Credits)
    * *Core Focus:* Postulates of quantum mechanics, time-independent Schrödinger wave equation derivation, operator algebra, particle-in-a-box solutions, harmonic oscillators, hydrogen-like atomic orbitals, Boltzmann distribution law, partition functions, and quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac/Bose-Einstein).

## 📂 Sub-Directory Architecture

To maintain optimal academic taxonomy, every course directory implements the following uniform classification structure:
* 📁 **`Books/`** — Standard peer-reviewed textbooks, reference volumes, and literary resources recommended by the departmental board.
* 📁 **`Notes/`** — High-resolution digitized manuscript lecture notes, structured analytical content, and academic review materials.
* 📁 **`Slides/`** — Official presentation materials, lecture slides, and visual aids provided during the instructional modules.

## 🤝 Collaborative Contribution Guidelines

Peer review and collaborative input are fundamental to maintaining the scholarly accuracy of this repository. Undergraduate scholars, faculty members, and alumni are strongly encouraged to contribute verified materials.

To propose updates or append resources:
1. **Fork** this repository to create a personal workspace.
2. Commit your documents into the precise course and asset category using explicit naming conventions (e.g., `CH_361_Lecture_04_IR_Spectroscopy.pdf`).
3. Submit a formal **Pull Request (PR)** detailing the source and validation of the appended literature.
